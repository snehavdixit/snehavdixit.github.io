News : Has the Large Hadron Collider Destroyed the World Yet?

I am currently a graduate student studying High Energy Physics at the University of Nebraska, Lincoln.

The Standard Model of Particle Physics is one of the most successful theories describing the basic building blocks of our universe. On 4 July 2012, the ATLAS and CMS experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, Geneva, jointly announced the discovery of the Higgs Boson. This particle is the last 'missing piece' in the Standard Model of Particle Physics.

However, many questions still remain unanswered. There are many phenomena that the Standard Model cannot explain. There exists Physics 'Beyond the Standard Model'. What are dark matter and dark energy? Why is there more matter than anti-matter in our universe? Why do neutrinos have mass?

These are some of the questions that High Energy Physics aims to answer.
